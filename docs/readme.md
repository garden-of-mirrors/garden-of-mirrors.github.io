## Introduction:

A "Vtuber" is someone who uses a virtual model to represent themselves online through video, especially in the context of livestreaming sites such as YouTube and Twitch. As opposed to a normalized perspective that would expect those livestreaming, recording videos, or otherwise using cameras for some purpose to be showing their exact physical face and voice, vtubers present themselves through a virtual model that often is motion-captured to reflect their physical motions, and may optionally use voice changing software to alter their voice according to their model.

While many major vtubers operate in organized idol groups, a multitude of independent vtubers exist that simply stream or record while using such a virtual avatar. The avatars themselves also vary: ranging from fully rendered 3D models, to 2.5D layered animations (often referred to as "2D models" or "live2D rigs" after the most common software used to do so), to something as simple as static 2D frames to be switched around as appropriate (often referred to as "PNGtubers").
  
  

"Genderqueerness" is the state of existence beyond strict cisgender norms: that being the idea that one's gender identity and expression, virtual and physical and otherwise, strictly aligns with gender assigned at birth at all times. For example, one might identify as a different gender from that assigned, a number of genders that isn't 1 such as "2", "0", "3/2", or even something negative...

Incidentally, presenting and organizing one's self to align in that regard physically is nontrivial, and doing so in a way that exposes that facet of one's self to the entire world through physical means is often intimidating at minimum, to outright dangerous at worst.


... Unsurprisingly, these two domains have a nontrivial overlap. 

!["Vtubers that are cishet: 0.0000000004%"](/assets/img/vtubers-cishet.png)

Obviously, this exaggerates a bit, not all vtubers are *gender*queer specifically, and not everyone who's genderqueer would be suited to using virtual models. However, they can be a useful tool to express one's true self or experiment with what that might be: it's definitely easier to change a drawing or model than your entire physical self.

It's just a shame there's no easy guide on how to simply mess around with these, start small streams with them, voice call others with them, and make those models, and nothing especially suited to doing so with the entire gender thing in the mix.

## Resources:

Depending on whether you're looking into 3D vtubing, PNGtubing (static/keyframed 2D), or live2D-style (tracked 2D) vtubing, the tools you will use to create your model rig and animate it with motion-capture software will vary. 

### 3D Models (.vrm)

[VRoid Studio](https://vroid.com/en/studio) is one of the more accessible tools for 3D modeling, with a dress-up game or character creator styled parameterized modeling tool. (Essentially, a base model with sliders to adjust it and modify the common elements, that allows custom textures as well as built-in presets to be imported.)

[VSeeFace](https://www.vseeface.icu/) is an open source face tracking tool, used for .vrm models exported from VRoid Studio and other 3D modeling software.
As many people - myself included - migrate to Linux with the increasing copilot-ification of Windows, VSeeFace does work with Linux machines, albeit with [a few workarounds detailed here](/resources-vseeface-linux.md).
